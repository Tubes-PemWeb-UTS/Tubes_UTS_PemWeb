Gunung Semeru



Mount Semeru or Mount Meru is a cone volcano in East Java, Indonesia. Mount Semeru is the highest mountain on the island of Java, with its peak Mahameru, 3,676 meters above sea level (masl). The mountain was formed by the subduction of the Indo-Australian Plate beneath the Eurasian Plate. Mount Semeru is also the third highest volcano in Indonesia after Mount Kerinci in Sumatra and Mount Rinjani in West Nusa Tenggara.The crater at the top of Mount Semeru is known as Jonggring Saloko.

Taman Nasional Alas Purwo



Alas Purwo National Park is a forest area that has a variety of ecosystem types that are classified as intact on the island of Java. Ecosystems range from coastal (coastal forest) to lowland rainforest, mangrove forest, bamboo forest, artificial savanna and plantation forest. The diversity of terrestrial flora in Alas Purwo National Park is high. It is known that more than 700 species of plants ranging from understory to tree level plants of various vegetation types/formations. Typical plants in this national park are Sawo Kecik (Manilkara kauki) and the protected species Sadeng (Livistoma rotundifolia).

Kawah Ijen



Ijen Crater is a natural tourist attraction in the form of a bluish-green acid lake with a diameter of about 700 meters and an area of 5,466 hectares. Ijen Crater has a depth of up to 200 meters and the volume of the lake can reach 36 million cubic meters. This lake was formed naturally due to the eruption of Mount Ijen which is still active until it finally forms a large indentation and becomes a lake.
